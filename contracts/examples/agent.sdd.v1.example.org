ASP Org specification records define governing design knowledge. Execution
records link to them instead of copying their fields.

** Purpose And Non-Goals
- Purpose: define how ASP Org contracts and templates remain parser-visible.
- Non-goal: record command transcripts or task progress in the SDD.
- Non-goal: duplicate Plan, ExecPlan, ADR, PRD, or BDD records.

** System Boundary
ASP Org owns reusable Org contracts and templates. Language providers own
parsing and contract evaluation. Agent skills own workflow instructions.

| Boundary | Owner | In Scope | Out Of Scope | Contract |
|----------+-------+----------+--------------+----------|
| contracts/templates | asp-org | contract structure and template projection | runtime command progress | agent.sdd.v1 |

** Stakeholders And Consumers
| Consumer | Need | Stability expectation | Failure impact |
|----------+------+-----------------------+----------------|
| agent | know which record owns design knowledge | stable | duplicated or stale plans |
| harness | validate Org structure through parser-owned facts | stable | template drift |

** Requirements And Constraints
- Requirement: templates must satisfy their governing contract.
- Requirement: execution records must link to SDD records through governing refs.
- Constraint: implementation progress remains in Plan or ExecPlan records.

** Quality Attributes
- Quality: semantic boundaries remain explicit.
- Quality: contracts stay parser-visible.
- Quality: execution state links back to specification records.

** Design Concerns
- Concern: plan records must not duplicate SDD, ADR, BDD, or PRD content.
- Concern: templates must not grow sections that their contract cannot validate.
- Constraint: execution plans link to this SDD through governing properties.

** Rollout And Migration

- Archive: remove stale template-only SDD sections.
